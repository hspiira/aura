  ENTERPRISE PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT SYSTEM
  Proposal for C-Suite Approval

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
-----------------
This document proposes an enterprise-grade performance management system that:
•	Aligns objectives to strategy across departments and roles
•	Combines quantitative (KPIs) and behavioral evaluation with calibration
•	Enforces a full audit trail and reward mapping for fairness and legal defensibility
•	Supports multi-year cycles and portfolio-based KPIs (insurance/broker context)

Ask: Approve the policy and governance preconditions below; endorse a phased rollout (Foundation → Pilot → Scale) over approximately 8–12 months, with an executive sponsor and pilot department.

Critical success factor: Governance and policy must be in place before the system goes live. Without signed executive policy and locked reward mapping, the system becomes ceremonial and will degrade within two cycles.


SECTION 1 — WHY: BUSINESS CASE & RISK OF INACTION
--------------------------------------------------

1.1 The Problem
•	Performance expectations and rewards need to be transparent, consistent, and defensible across the organization.
•	Multi-department, role-based hierarchy and multi-year cycles require a single system of record—not spreadsheets or ad hoc process.
•	Quantitative and behavioral evaluation must be separated, weighted by role, and calibrated to avoid rating inflation and bias.

1.2 Risk of Inaction
If we allow:
•	Manual scoring without evidence or audit
•	Silent target changes mid-cycle
•	Flexible weight manipulation or late-year bulk updates
the system (and trust in it) will degrade within two cycles. Governance must be stricter than culture initially.

1.3 Benefits
•	Transparency: Clear objectives, locked timelines, and visible calibration
•	Fairness: Reward mapping and role-based weights applied consistently
•	Legal defensibility: Full audit trail, immutable closed scores, amendment protocol
•	Analytics: Multi-year trends, promotion readiness, attrition risk, rating distribution


SECTION 2 — VISION, SCOPE & PRINCIPLES
--------------------------------------

2.1 Scope (What We Are Proposing)
This model assumes:
•	Multi-department organization
•	Role-based hierarchy
•	Multi-year performance cycles
•	Quantitative + behavioral scoring
•	Calibration and reward mapping
•	Full audit trail

2.2 Architectural Principles
•	Separation of calculation engine from storage
•	No manual override without audit
•	Configurable weighting by role
•	Strict workflow enforcement
•	Time-stamped updates only

2.3 Critical Governance Rules (High Level)
1.	Objectives locked after Q1.
2.	No update for 90 days → auto-flag.
3.	Weight sum must equal 100%.
4.	Score auto-calculated — not manually entered.
5.	Closed objectives immutable.


SECTION 3 — PRECONDITIONS: POLICY & GOVERNANCE FIRST
----------------------------------------------------
The system must NOT go live unless all conditions below are satisfied.

3.1 Executive Policy Signed
•	Written performance policy approved by executive leadership.
•	Defines: weight distribution rules, review cadence, reward mapping, consequences for non-compliance.
•	Without policy, software becomes ceremonial.

3.2 Role & KPI Alignment Complete
•	Every role has: defined performance dimensions, default weight distribution, mandatory objective templates.
•	Department KPIs mapped to corporate KPIs. No orphan objectives allowed.

3.3 Reward Mapping Finalized
Reward bands must be configured before cycle starts. Example:
Final Score    Outcome
≥90           Tier A Bonus
80–89         Tier B Bonus
70–79         Standard
<70           PIP
If reward logic is undefined, scoring loses integrity.

3.4 Annual Operational Calendar (When Things Happen)
Month 0 (Pre-Cycle): Executive KPI confirmation, department KPI cascading, template publication, reward band confirmation.
Month 1 (Planning Window): Staff draft objectives, supervisor approvals, baseline snapshots captured, objectives locked.
Quarter 1 End: Mandatory update, performance variance report, at-risk objectives flagged.
Mid-Year Review: Formal review session, preliminary scoring, calibration check.
Quarter 3 Review: Progress validation, PIP identification (if required).
Year-End: Final updates locked, scoring engine runs, behavioral scoring, calibration session, final summaries, reward mapping executed.


SECTION 4 — HOW IT WORKS (CONCEPTUAL)
--------------------------------------

4.1 End-to-End Flow
Organization → Departments → Roles → Users (with reporting lines)
     ↓
Performance Cycle (e.g. 2026 Annual) + Dimensions (Financial, Operational, Client, Compliance, Behavioral, Innovation)
     ↓
Objectives (per user, per cycle): measurable goals with target, weight, approval, and lifecycle
     ↓
Objective Updates (periodic progress) + Evidence (proof) → Objective Scores (calculated, locked)
     ↓
Behavioral Indicators + Behavioral Scores (e.g. 1–5 scale, weighted)
     ↓
Review Sessions (quarterly, mid-year, final) → Performance Summary (final weighted score, rating band, comments)
     ↓
Calibration Session (department/org) + Reward Policy (score → bonus/promotion/PIP)
     ↓
Audit Log (all changes for defensibility)

4.2 Objective Lifecycle (Simplified)
Draft → Submitted → Approved → Active → Under Review → Closed
(Rejected and Amended are supported with justification and audit.)

4.3 Portfolio-Based KPIs (Insurance/Broker Context)
Replace vague retention with structured metrics:
•	Revenue Retention Rate = (Renewed Premium Value / Total Premium at Start) × 100
•	Net Growth Rate = ((New Business Premium - Lost Premium) / Baseline Premium) × 100
•	Claims Ratio (if applicable) = (Total Claims Paid / Total Premium) × 100
•	Policy Renewal Lead Time = average days before expiry renewal completed
•	Client Concentration Risk = Top 5 Clients Premium / Total Premium

KPI governance: All financial KPIs must pull from system of record. No manual financial input. Adjust for reassigned portfolios. Economic shocks require executive-wide adjustment, not individual edits.


SECTION 5 — GOVERNANCE IN PRACTICE
----------------------------------

5.1 Templates & Validation
•	Objective templates are immutable after cycle start; any change requires versioning.
•	SMART validation before submission: title length, KPI type, target in range, weight sum = 100%, dates in cycle, baseline snapshot when required.
•	Anti-gaming: new target cannot be >20% below last achievement without justification; custom objectives max 30% weight; mandatory templates cannot be removed; free-text objectives prohibited unless Behavioral.

5.2 Baseline Snapshots
•	Baseline frozen at cycle start (or at objective approval for mid-cycle join).
•	Baseline cannot be edited. Portfolio reassignment → adjustment record, prorated target, audit log. No manual baseline unless dual approval.

5.3 Lifecycle Enforcement
Mandatory transitions: Draft → Submitted → Approved → Active → Under Review → Closed.
Hard controls: Objectives lock after Q1. No updates 90 days → auto-flag. Closed objectives immutable. Amendments require written justification, supervisor approval, HR visibility, audit log.

5.4 Calibration & Abuse Prevention
•	Distribution monitoring: score histogram per department, mean vs standard deviation, manager variance. Flag excessive clustering above 90% and outlier managers.
•	Override logging: any manual score override requires written justification, HR approval, audit record.
•	Enforcement: failure to complete quarterly update → weight reduced by 5% or flag as compliance breach.

5.5 Pre-Production Go-Live Checklist
System cannot go live unless:
•	Templates versioned, reward mapping tested, baseline snapshot automation verified
•	SMART engine tested with invalid cases, weight enforcement validated
•	Calibration dashboard functioning, audit logging verified
•	Role-based permissions enforced, test cycle executed with pilot group
•	Executive sign-off documented

5.6 Structural Warning (Reinforced)
If you allow manual scoring, silent target changes, flexible weight manipulation, or late-year bulk updates, the system will degrade within 2 cycles. Governance must be stricter than culture initially.


SECTION 6 — RECOMMENDATIONS & ENHANCEMENTS (Industry Practice)
--------------------------------------------------------------
These additions strengthen the blueprint and align with patterns used in Workday, SuccessFactors, Lattice, and similar systems.

6.1 Calibration: Visibility Over Forced Distribution
•	Do NOT enforce a forced bell curve (fixed % per rating band). Research shows forced distribution increases gaming and undermines collaboration.
•	Use calibration as visibility and conversation: score histograms, mean vs standard deviation, manager variance, heatmaps.
•	Optional: publish soft guidelines (e.g. "expect ~X% in top band") for discussion only — never system-enforced quotas.
•	Flag excessive clustering above 90% and outlier managers; require narrative justification, not forced redistribution.

6.2 Continuous Feedback / Check-Ins (Optional)
•	Consider a first-class Feedback or CheckIn entity: manager ↔ employee, tied to cycle and optionally to objective.
•	Enables ongoing conversation and reduces reliance on year-end evidence only.
•	Schema sketch: feedback_sessions (id, user_id, cycle_id, reviewer_id, type, date, comment, created_at).

6.3 Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)
•	Add minimal RBAC early: permissions, role_permissions, user_roles (with optional scope_department_id).
•	Define who can: approve objectives, run calibration, view audit logs, override scores, manage templates.

6.4 Notifications and Deadlines
•	"No update for 90 days → auto-flag" and "objectives lock after Q1" require executed actions.
•	Define notification_rules (event_type, recipient_role, channel, template) and notification_log. Events: quarterly_update_due, objective_locked, calibration_scheduled, review_completed, amendment_requested. At least one channel (email or in-app) must be implemented.

6.5 Integrations and System of Record
•	Add data_sources or integration_systems and link baseline_snapshots and objective actuals. Baseline and actuals for financial KPIs should reference system of record; no manual financial input without dual approval and audit.

6.6 Objective Versioning
•	"Amended" state and "old target preserved" require traceable versioning. Add objective_versions table (or version column + audit history). Recalculation uses latest approved target; previous versions remain for audit and legal defensibility.


SECTION 7 — IMPLEMENTATION: PHASED ROLLOUT & ASK
------------------------------------------------

7.1 Governance and Product as One Roadmap
•	Policy first: Get Executive Policy signed and published before building.
•	Reward mapping: Lock reward bands and document in system (RewardPolicy) before first cycle. Run dry run with prior-year or synthetic data to validate.

7.2 Phased Rollout (Chronological)
•	Foundation (2–3 months): Policy, templates, role/dimension weights, reward bands, RBAC, one pilot department.
•	Pilot (3–4 months): 1–2 departments, full cycle (objectives → updates → scoring → calibration → summary), baseline automation, SMART validation.
•	Scale (2–4 months): Roll out by department/division; calibration dashboard, audit review, analytics.
•	Optimize (ongoing): Optional feedback/check-ins, integrations, refinements from calibration and HR feedback.
•	Pilot with a group that has clear, measurable KPIs (e.g. one broker/portfolio team) so baseline snapshots and system-of-record integration are tested early.

7.3 Build Order (Recommended)
1.	Org layer + performance framework (org, department, role, user, cycle, dimensions, role-dimension weights).
2.	Objectives + lifecycle (objectives, templates, statuses, approval, lock rules).
3.	SMART validation + baseline snapshots (validation engine, baseline table, data source linkage).
4.	Updates + evidence + scoring engine (objective updates, evidence, score calculation, caps, edge cases).
5.	Behavioral layer (indicators, behavioral scores, weighting into final score).
6.	Review + summary (review sessions, performance summary, workflow).
7.	Calibration + audit (calibration sessions, distribution reports, audit log, immutable close).
8.	RBAC + notifications (permissions, reminder/notification execution).
9.	Analytics (star schema, ETL from OLTP, dashboards).

7.4 Change Management and Adoption
•	Executive sponsor: One C-level or VP who runs their own objectives and reviews in the system and references it in all-hands.
•	Pilot feedback: After first pilot cycle, collect feedback and implement at least one process or UX change so the organization sees that input matters.
•	Training by persona: Separate short flows for employee (set objectives, submit updates), manager (approve, score, comment), HR (templates, calibration, audit), analysts (reporting).
•	Messaging: Frame the system as "transparent expectations and fair rewards, fewer year-end surprises" — not surveillance.

7.5 Release Gates
Implement "no manual override without audit" and "amendments require justification + approval + audit" in code and process from day one. Use the Pre-Production Go-Live Checklist (5.5) as real release gates: no go-live without reward mapping tested, baseline automation verified, and audit logging verified.

  APPENDIX A — DATA MODEL (ENTITIES & LIFECYCLE)

A.1 Organizational Structure Layer
•	Organization (legal entity; multi-tenant, policy scope)
•	Department (parent, head; reporting rollups, calibration grouping)
•	Role (department, level, is managerial; default dimensions, templates)
•	User (role, department, supervisor; objectives, reporting lines)

•	PerformanceCycle (name, start/end, status, review frequency)
•	PerformanceDimension (e.g. Financial, Operational, Client, Compliance, Behavioral, Innovation; is quantitative, default weight)
•	RoleDimensionWeight (role, dimension, weight %) — e.g. executives 50% financial, junior 70% operational

A.3 Objective Layer
•	Objective (user, cycle, dimension, title, description, KPIType, target, weight, dates, status, locked/approved)
•	ObjectiveUpdate (progress updates, actual value, comment, submitted by)
•	ObjectiveEvidence (file, description, uploaded by/at)
•	ObjectiveScore (achievement %, weighted score, calculated at, locked)

A.4 Behavioral Evaluation Layer
•	BehavioralIndicator (dimension, name, description, rating scale definition)
•	BehavioralScore (user, cycle, indicator, rating 1–5, manager comment)

A.5 Review & Workflow Layer
•	ReviewSession (user, cycle, reviewer, type, status, scheduled/completed date)
•	PerformanceSummary (user, cycle, quantitative score, behavioral score, final weighted score, final rating band, manager/employee comment, HR approval)

A.6 Calibration & Governance Layer
•	CalibrationSession (cycle, department, conducted by, date, notes)
•	RewardPolicy (min/max score, reward type, reward value)

A.7 Audit & Integrity Layer
•	AuditLog (entity type, entity id, action, old/new value, changed by, changed at)

A.8 Objective Lifecycle States (Detail)
Draft → Submitted → Rejected | Approved → Active → At Risk (system) | Completed → Under Review → Closed. Amended: requires justification, supervisor approval, audit; old target preserved, versioning applied.


================================================================================
  APPENDIX B — TECHNICAL BLUEPRINT (SQL, ER, SCORING, ANALYTICS)
================================================================================

B.1 SQL-Level Database Schema (PostgreSQL — Core Tables)
Organizational Layer: organizations, departments, roles, users (with org_id, department_id, role_id, supervisor_id).
Performance Framework: performance_cycles, performance_dimensions, role_dimension_weights.
Objectives: objectives (user_id, performance_cycle_id, dimension_id, title, description, kpi_type, target_value, unit_of_measure, weight, start_date, end_date, status, approved_at, approved_by, locked_at). Constraint: weight > 0.
Objective Updates & Evidence: objective_updates, objective_evidence.
Scoring: objective_scores (achievement_percentage, weighted_score, calculated_at, locked).
Behavioral: behavioral_indicators, behavioral_scores (rating 1–5).
Final Summary: performance_summaries (quantitative_score, behavioral_score, final_weighted_score, final_rating_band, manager_comment, employee_comment, hr_approved).
Audit: audit_logs (entity_type, entity_id, action, old_value JSONB, new_value JSONB, changed_by, changed_at).

B.2 ER Structure (Logical)
Organization → Departments → Roles → Users → Objectives → ObjectiveUpdates, ObjectiveEvidence, ObjectiveScores. Users → BehavioralScores. PerformanceCycle → Objectives, BehavioralScores, PerformanceSummary. One User → Many Objectives; One Objective → Many Updates, One Final Score; One User per Cycle → One PerformanceSummary.

B.3 Scoring Algorithm
•	Achievement % = (Actual / Target) × 100 (or Target/Actual for inverse metrics). Cap: 0 ≤ % ≤ 150.
•	Weighted Score = (Achievement % × Objective Weight) / 100. Total Quant Score = SUM(Weighted Scores).
•	Behavioral % = (Average Rating / 5) × 100; apply dimension weight (e.g. 20%).
•	Final Score = (Quantitative Score × 0.8) + (Behavioral Score × 0.2) (example 80/20).
Edge cases: No updates → flag, default 0 unless manager override with justification. Target revised mid-year → old preserved, recalculation uses latest approved target, amendment record. Objective completed early → freeze updates, calculate immediately. Partial employment → prorate weight by days in cycle. Weight sum ≠ 100% → reject approval. Zero target → use milestone-based scoring, no division.

B.4 Multi-Year Analytics Model
Separate OLTP from analytics. Never overwrite objective_scores or performance_summaries. Star schema: fact_performance_summary (user_id, department_id, role_id, cycle_year, quantitative_score, behavioral_score, final_score, rating_band); dimensions: dim_user, dim_department, dim_role, dim_cycle, dim_time. Use cases: performance trend 3–5 years, rating distribution (inflation by department), promotion readiness (e.g. 3 consecutive > 85%, no violations), attrition risk (high performer + low reward mapping).


================================================================================
  APPENDIX C — FULL GOVERNANCE SCHEMA (TEMPLATES, BASELINE, SMART)
================================================================================

C.1 ObjectiveTemplate Table
objective_templates (id, code UNIQUE, title, description, dimension_id, kpi_type, formula_type, data_source, default_target, min_target, max_target, default_weight, difficulty_factor, mandatory_for_role, allow_target_override, allow_weight_override, requires_baseline_snapshot, is_active, created_by, created_at). Governance: templates immutable after cycle start; versioning for changes; target ranges and data source declared; difficulty factor mandatory for financial KPIs.

C.2 SMART Validation Engine (Pseudocode)
IF title.length < 10 → reject. IF kpi_type IS NULL → reject. IF target_value IS NULL → reject. IF target_value < template.min_target OR > template.max_target → reject. IF weight_total != 100 → reject. IF start_date < cycle.start_date OR end_date > cycle.end_date → reject. IF requires_baseline_snapshot = TRUE AND baseline_snapshot_missing → reject. Anti-gaming: previous cycle — new target cannot be >20% below last achievement without justification; custom objectives max 30% weight; mandatory templates cannot be removed; free-text prohibited unless Behavioral.

C.3 BaselineSnapshot Table
baseline_snapshots (id, user_id, performance_cycle_id, template_id, baseline_value, snapshot_date, data_source, created_at). Rules: snapshot on cycle start or objective approval (mid-cycle join); baseline cannot be edited; portfolio reassignment → adjustment record, prorated target, audit; no manual baseline unless dual approval.
